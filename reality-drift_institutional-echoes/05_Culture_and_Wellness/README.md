# Culture and Wellness

This directory contains analyses of contemporary wellness culture as an optimized symbolic system rather than a purely therapeutic or restorative domain.

The focus is on how practices intended to promote health, balance, and self-regulation are shaped by platforms, incentives, and optimization pressures that thin embodied experience while preserving the appearance of care.

---

## Contents

- RD-ECHO_2025_Marlowe_Somatic-Thinness-in-Optimized-Wellness-Culture.pdf  
  An examination of how modern wellness culture increasingly emphasizes measurable, repeatable, and marketable signals of health at the expense of lived somatic depth.

---

## Purpose

The materials in this directory exist to:

- Study how wellness practices become abstracted into routines, metrics, and identities  
- Examine the gap between symbolic self-care and embodied regulation  
- Observe how optimization transforms care into performance  

These documents are intended for cultural researchers, wellness practitioners, designers, and observers of modern self-regulation systems.

## Notes

Wellness systems rarely fail loudly.

They continue to function, circulate, and scale even as they lose contact with the bodily signals they claim to restore.

When optimization prioritizes consistency, shareability, and identity alignment, somatic experience becomes thinner without disappearing.

These materials document that thinning as a structural condition rather than an individual shortcoming.

Institutional names and formats may be fictionalized or composited to reflect systemic dynamics rather than specific organizations.
